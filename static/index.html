Kerak (24-11-2022)

Kerak is a city known for its impressive castle. The Kerak Castle, a crusader castle, is one of the largest and most preserved in the region. The city itself is steeped in history and offers a glimpse into the past. The local cuisine is also worth trying, especially the famous Kerak lamb.

Shobak Castle - Montreal (25-11-2022)

Shobak Castle, also known as Montreal, is a crusader castle in Jordan. It's a bit off the beaten path, but well worth the visit. The castle is perched on the side of a mountain and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The castle itself is a fascinating piece of history and a great place to explore.

Wadi Musa (25-11-2022)

Wadi Musa, or "Valley of Moses", is the gateway to the ancient city of Petra. The town itself is charming, with friendly locals and a variety of restaurants and shops. It's the perfect place to rest and recharge before or after exploring Petra. The local cuisine is a must-try, especially the traditional Jordanian dishes.

The Treasury (26-11-2022)

The Treasury, or Al-Khazneh, is one of the most stunning sights in Petra. Carved out of a sandstone rock face, it's an architectural marvel that leaves you in awe. The intricate details and the sheer size of the monument are truly breathtaking. It's a sight that's worth the journey to Petra alone.

Nabatean Theatre (26-11-2022)

The Nabatean Theatre in Petra is a testament to the architectural prowess of the ancient Nabateans. Carved into the side of a mountain, the theatre could seat over 6000 people. It's a fascinating place to visit, offering a glimpse into the cultural life of the ancient city.

Obelisks (26-11-2022)

The Obelisks in Petra are a sight to behold. These towering structures stand as a testament to the city's rich history and the skill of its ancient builders. The obelisks are located on a high plateau and offer a stunning view of the surrounding landscape. It's a bit of a climb to get there, but the view is well worth it.

Haut-lieu du Sacrifice (26-11-2022)

The Haut-lieu du Sacrifice, or High Place of Sacrifice, is one of the most important religious sites in Petra. The site offers a panoramic view of the city and the surrounding mountains. It's a place of quiet and reflection, and a reminder of the spiritual significance of this ancient city.

Lion Fountain (26-11-2022)

The Lion Fountain is one of the lesser-known attractions in Petra, but it's worth a visit. The fountain, carved into the rock, is believed to have been part of a Nabatean water system. The lion's head, from which water once flowed, is still clearly visible. It's a fascinating piece of history tucked away in the heart of Petra.
